Questions

  1. Prove that $P \land (Q \lor R)$ is equivalent to $(P \land Q) \lor (P \land R)$.

    Does this seem familiar? Something similar is true in basic algebra: $P \cdot (Q + R) = P \cdot Q + P \cdot R$. This is known as the distributive property (see link for a full list of rules). It turns out, boolean logic and arithmetic share the same underlying mathematical structure. You can learn more by reading the Wikipedia article on boolean algebra, or taking a class in abstract algebra.
